A Tribute to Hiroshi Yamauchi

The man who transformed Nintendo

  • 1927 - Yamauchi is born on the 7th of November in Kyoto

  • 1945 - Yamauchi attended Waseda University after the end of WWII

  • 1948 - Yamauchi is asked to take over the company Nintendo, as his grandfather, who was the 2nd president of Nintendo, had suffered a stroke

  • 1949 - Yamauchi’s grandfather passes away, leaving Yamauchi in charge

  • 1959 - Yamauchi makes an agreement with Disney to create plastic playing cards for families, which led to Nintendo gaining a near monopoly on the playing card market in Japan – after visiting other playing card companies in the U.S., however, Yamauchi decides to diversify his company as it would expand their business opportunities.

  • 1977 - Nintendo released its first game console, the Color TV-Game

  • 1981 - Yamauchi pushed forward Donkey Kong, a project by designer Shigeru Miyamoto, which became a hit

  • 1983 - A family game console (Fanicom) was released in Japan, a console that Yamauchi was extremely confident in, which led him to push for a more artistic direction for video games

  • 1985 - The Fanicom was released in America as the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), and a second generation, the Super Nintendo Entertainment System, is released in 1990 in JP and 1992 in Europe and America

  • 1996 - Nintendo 64 is released, Nintendo’s 3D console. At this time, Yamauchi began to discuss potentially retiring.

  • 2001 - The GameCube is released as a console solely for gameplay, which Yamauchi pushed to be more affordable so that it can be more accessible.

  • 2002 - Yamauchi steps down as Nintendo’s president.

  • 2013 - Yamauchi passes away as a result of complications from pneumonia.
